HTML Favicon

A favicon (short for "favorite icon") is a small icon that represents a website. It is typically displayed in the browser's address bar, next to the page title in the browser tab, and in bookmarks or favorites lists. Favicons help users quickly identify and recognize a website.

How to Add a Favicon to Your Website

To add a favicon to your website, you can use the following HTML code within the <head> section of your HTML document: 

<link rel="icon" href="path/to/favicon.ico" type="image/x-icon" />

Replace "path/to/favicon.ico" with the actual path to your favicon file. The favicon file can be in various formats such as .ico, .png, or .svg, but .ico is the most widely supported format for favicons.

Best Practices for Favicons

  • Use a simple and recognizable design for your favicon.
  • Ensure that the favicon is clear and visible even at small sizes (16x16 pixels).
  • Consider creating multiple sizes of your favicon (e.g., 16x16, 32x32, 48x48) for better compatibility across different devices and browsers.
  • Test your favicon on various browsers and devices to ensure it displays correctly.

By adding a favicon to your website, you can enhance your site's branding and make it easier for users to identify and return to your site.
